How to Change a Carburetor

  • 32 Like
  • 12 Made
  • 6 Want to Make
Tag Products

Most of us gear heads can change a carburetor in our sleep but to a first timer it can be quite intimidating. So i have listed 8 steps to help get you through it.

    Materials

  • New carburetor base gasket.
  • Vacuum lines and cotter pins if needed
  • New carburetor base gasket.
  • New air filter

    Tools

  • Sae or metric combination wrenches
  • Rags
  • Fender cover
  • A pair of safety glasses
  • Regular and needle nose pliers
  • Step 1

    Put on your safety glasses and disconnect the battery. Also with todays modern technology just about everybody has a camera on their phone so take a few pictures to use for reference before removing anything from the carburetor. They will come in handy for reinstalling.

  • Step 2

    Remove the air cleaner from the carburetor (check air filter for replacement) and put a rag under the fuel lines to catch any spilling of the fuel then remove the fuel lines from the carburetor.

  • Step 3

    Disconnect the throttle linkage..Note: With an automatic transmission you might have a kick down cable below the throttle linkage. This is where a picture for reference will come in handy during reassembly. Also remove any vacuum lines. This is a good time to inspect the rubber lines for cracks or If their hard and brittle, replace them.

  • Step 4

    Remove the nuts or bolts that secure the carburetor to the intake manifold (counter clockwise to remove). Lift the carburetor straight up and keep it level to avoid spilling any fuel. Once its removed stuff some rags into the open ports on the intake manifold to keep anything from falling inside.

  • Step 5

    Clean the gasket surface on the intake manifold. Be sure the bottom of the carburetor surface is also clean and smooth. If it needs cleaning turn it over inside a pan large enough to hold the carburetor due to the gas draining out.

  • Step 6

    Remove the rags from the intake manifold ports and install the new gasket (dry) no gasket sealer needed.

  • Step 7

    Reinstall the carburetor and the nuts/bolts and tighten them down, if it's secured with 4 nut/bolts tighten them in a crisscross pattern until firmly snugged down (do not over tighten). Reinstall the throttle linkage, vacuum lines,and the fuel lines (once again tighten till snugged down do not over tighten them).

  • Step 8

    At this point check your engine area for any rags or tools and remove them. Reconnect the battery and start the car. Check your fuel lines while its running to make sure there are no leaks. Then reinstall your air cleaner.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is Workshop?

    Workbench is the digital home for your tool collection. Organize your tools, explore new additions and find inspiring projects to put your tools to the test.

  • What can I do in the Workshop?

    There are three main areas in your Workshop. Shop TalkTalk about tools, projects or anything else with your fellow tool enthusiasts. Tool Chest Find, manage and organize your personal tool collection. Projects Explore an extensive library of projects to complete or add your own to inspire others.

  • How do I add tools to my Tool Chest?

    In your Tool Chest, it's as simple as clicking 'Add a Tool' in the respective 'Drawer'. If you see a tool anywhere else in the Workshop you can add it to your collection by clicking the right action. If you own a tool, click the checkmark , the heart if you want it, and give it a thumbs-up if you simply like it.

  • What's the difference between owning, liking and wanting tools?

    Any one of these actions builds out your tool chest but there is a distinct difference between them. Own a ToolYou own this tool, it's in your personal collection and ready to use at a moments notice. Want a ToolYou don't yet own this tool but someday it will be yours. Think of it as your personal tool wishlist, and who knows, if you're nice someone may just get it for you. Like a ToolMaybe there's a tool you admire but have no personal use for. Feel free to like it anyway, even tools appreciate admiration.

  • I make awesome stuff, how do I show off my work?

    Showing off is easy, teaching others is a little more involved but much more rewarding. If you want to brag to the world, just post in Shop Talk. Feel free to add some images, the tools you used, and someone will surely notice your handiwork. If you want to teach and inspire others head to the projects section and add your own, step-by-step. Adding a cool project is a great way to gain some followers.

  • I'm ready to get my hands dirty, how do I find new stuff to do?

    Simple, click on the projects section and go to 'Explore Projects'. Browse through and find the perfect project for your next conquest.

  • How do I view the tools of other users?

    All around the Workshop there are others just like you managing their own tools and working on projects. Any time you see a user just click on the link below their name where it says their tool count. You can then browse their tool collection, see what they have, what they want and what they like.
